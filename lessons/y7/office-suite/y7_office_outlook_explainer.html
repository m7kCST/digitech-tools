Your School Email App

Used in Every Workplace

Outlook is the standard email app in schools, hospitals, law firms and businesses worldwide. Knowing how to use it - and use it well - is a professional skill.

📅

Outlook also includes a Calendar for scheduling, a People section for contacts, and Tasks for to-do lists. It's a full communication hub.

Tone Matters Here

Unlike texting or social media, email is a formal written record. A poorly written email to a teacher or employer can cause real problems. Tone, structure and language all matter.

Key Terms - Know These 📍

To
recipient@school.edu.au

The person you're sending the email to. Double-check this before sending - sending to the wrong person is embarrassing and can't be undone.

CC
Carbon Copy

Sends a copy to someone who needs to know but doesn't need to reply. Everyone in the email can see who is CC'd. Use sparingly.

BCC
Blind Carbon Copy

Like CC but hidden - the other recipients can't see who is BCC'd. Used for large group emails where people don't need each other's addresses.

Subject Line
Clear, specific, relevant

The title of your email. It should tell the reader exactly what the email is about before they open it. "Hi" is not a subject line.

Reply vs Reply All
Think before clicking

Reply goes to the sender only. Reply All goes to everyone on the email. Only use Reply All when everyone genuinely needs your response.

Forward
→ Forward

Sends a received email on to a new person. Always add a short note explaining why you're forwarding it - don't just forward with no context.

Attachment
📎 Attach File

A file sent with the email. Always mention the attachment in the email body - don't send files without explaining what they are.

Signature
Your name + year group

A sign-off block at the end of every email. Should include your name, year group, and school. Set this up in Settings so it adds automatically.

Tone & Etiquette 🎭

📝

Subject Lines

Be specific. A good subject line tells the reader exactly what the email is about before they open it.

✕ "Hi" / "Question" / "Help"
✓ "Question about Week 8 template - Year 9 DigiTech"
👋

Greetings

Use the person's correct title and name. Start with "Dear" for formal emails. End the greeting with a comma.

✕ "Hey" / "Yo" / no greeting at all
✓ "Dear Mr Miller," / "Dear Ms Kelly,"
💬

Tone

Write like you're speaking to a professional adult - polite, clear and direct. No slang, no abbreviations, no emoji.

✕ "can u send me the sheet thx"
✓ "Could you please send me the worksheet? Thank you."
✍️

Sign-offs

Always end with a polite sign-off, your full name and year group. Don't leave emails unsigned.

✕ "thanks" / "bye" / nothing
✓ "Kind regards, / Isaac Miller / Year 9 DigiTech"
⏱️

Response Time

Reply to emails within a reasonable time. Don't leave teachers or classmates waiting days for a reply when it only takes 2 minutes.

✓ Reply within 24 hours for school emails
📎

Attachments

Always mention the attachment in the email body. Name files correctly before attaching. Check you've actually attached it before sending.

✓ "Please find my assignment attached."
✕ Sending an attachment with no explanation

Do This / Never Do This 🚦

❌ Never do this
  • Send emails with no subject line
  • Use slang, emoji or abbreviations
  • Reply All to a whole-school email
  • Forward emails without context
  • Send without checking spelling
  • Leave emails unsigned
  • CC your whole class for no reason
  • Send angry emails - write it, wait, then decide
✅ Always do this
  • Write a clear, specific subject line
  • Use correct title - Mr / Ms / Mrs / Dr
  • Re-read before sending
  • Mention attachments in the body
  • Reply within 24 hours
  • Sign off with your name + year group
  • Check the To field before hitting send
  • Use the Email Writing Tool if unsure

The Rules That Actually Matter ⭐

⭐ Golden Rules

1

A subject line is not optional. Every email needs one. It should tell the reader exactly what the email is about in under 10 words.

2

Always use the correct title - Mr, Ms, Mrs, Dr. Getting someone's title wrong is rude, even if it's accidental.

3

Check the To field before you hit send. Sending to the wrong person is embarrassing and cannot be unsent.

4

Never use Reply All unless every person on the email genuinely needs your reply. Replying All to a whole-school email is a fast way to get in trouble.

5

Emails are a permanent written record. Write as if your principal might read it - because they sometimes do.

6

If you're unsure how to phrase something, use the Email Writing Tool on the class site before sending. It only takes 30 seconds.

When Things Go Wrong 🚨

🚨 Common Mistakes

Sent to wrong person

You can't unsend it. Email the right person immediately and explain the mistake politely. Don't ignore it hoping no one notices.

Forgot the attachment

Send a follow-up email immediately: "Apologies - I forgot to attach the file. Please find it attached now." Don't just re-send the whole email without explanation.

Email in Junk/Spam

Check your Junk Email folder regularly. Mark legitimate emails as "Not Junk" so they go to your inbox in future. Teachers' emails sometimes land here.

Tone came across wrong

Before sending an emotional email, save it as a draft and re-read it an hour later. If you're unsure, ask a classmate or parent to read it first.

Can't find an old email

Use Ctrl+F to search. Search by sender name, subject keywords or date range. Check Deleted Items if you accidentally deleted it.
