What Even Is Excel? 🤔

📋

A Spreadsheet App

Excel is a grid of boxes called cells. You put numbers, words, or formulas in each cell. It's like a super-powered calculator that organises data at the same time.

🏢

Used Everywhere

Budgets, rosters, science data, sports stats, business reports — if someone needs to organise numbers, they're probably using Excel (or something like it).

⚡

Formulas Do the Work

Instead of adding up 50 numbers on a calculator, you type one formula and Excel does it instantly. Change any number and the answer updates automatically.

📁

Files = Workbooks

An Excel file is called a workbook. Inside it you can have multiple sheets (tabs at the bottom). Today you'll use Sheet1 and Sheet2.

Cell Addresses — The GPS of Excel 📍

Column
A B C D …

Goes left to right. Labelled with letters. Column A is always on the far left.

Row
1 2 3 4 …

Goes top to bottom. Labelled with numbers. Row 1 is always at the top.

Cell Address
B3

Column letter first, then row number. B3 = column B, row 3. Click any cell to see its address in the Name Box (top left).

Cell Range
A2:A8

A group of cells. The colon means "from … to". A2:A8 means every cell from A2 down to A8.

Formula Bar
fx =SUM(…)

The bar above the grid. Shows exactly what's inside the selected cell — including the full formula, not just the result.

Sheet Tab
Sheet1 Sheet2

Tabs at the bottom of the screen. One workbook can have many sheets. Double-click a tab to rename it.

Formulas — Making Excel Do the Work ⚡

ME DOING MATHS
🧮
VS. EXCEL DOING MATHS
=SUM(A2:A6)

Adds up a range of numbers

Instead of adding every number yourself, SUM does it instantly. Give it a range and it handles the rest.

=start every formula with this

(
A2:A6the range
)

Try it: Click an empty cell, type =SUM( then drag to select your numbers. Excel writes the range for you.

The Rules That Actually Matter ⭐

⭐ Golden Rules

1

Every formula starts with = — no equals sign, no formula. It just shows as text.

2

A range uses a colon — A2:A6 means from A2 to A6. Excel reads it as "everything between these two cells."

3

Cell names = column letter + row number. B3 = column B, row 3. That's the whole rule.

4

Save constantly — Ctrl+S. If your laptop dies and you haven't saved, that's on you.

5

Never merge cells without asking your teacher first. It causes problems that are annoying to fix.

Error Translator 🚨

🚨 When Things Go Wrong

#VALUE!

Translation: "You gave me words when I wanted numbers." Check your formula range — one of the cells probably has text in it (like a heading row you accidentally included).

#REF!

Translation: "I can't find the cells you pointed me to." A cell your formula referenced has been deleted. Press Ctrl+Z to undo and try again.

Shows as text

Translation: "You forgot the = at the start." Delete the cell contents and retype starting with =

Result = 0

Translation: "The cells in my range are empty." Click each cell in your formula range to confirm it actually contains a number.
